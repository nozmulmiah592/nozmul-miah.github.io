About Me

I'm a final-year Mechanical Engineering student at Aston University, based in Birmingham, UK. Over four years of study I've progressed from learning fundamental design principles to independently delivering complex, multi-disciplinary engineering projects — from portable biomedical devices to augmented reality systems for CNC machine monitoring.

My work sits at the intersection of traditional mechanical engineering and emerging digital technologies. I'm equally comfortable creating parametric CAD models in SolidWorks and writing C# scripts in Unity for the Meta Quest 3 platform. I believe this combination of practical engineering grounding and digital fluency is what Industry 4.0 manufacturing environments need.

Before university, I completed a BTEC Extended Diploma in Engineering (D*D*M) at Aston University Engineering Academy, which gave me a strong practical foundation in workshop processes, materials, and engineering science.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy the gym, football, and badminton. I'm currently seeking graduate engineering roles and degree apprenticeships in the mechanical, rail, and utilities sectors.

Finite Element Analysis of a Stepped Shaft

FEA Engineer

Investigated stress concentration in an axially loaded stepped shaft using a fully parametric geometry. Numerical results verified through mesh convergence studies, hand calculations, and comparison with published reference data.

This analysis highlighted the sensitivity of FE results to geometric discontinuities and local mesh density. Progressive refinement demonstrated convergence of peak stress and distribution along the critical edge. The calculated Kt ≈ 1.39 showed close agreement with the published reference (Kt = 1.43), confirming the modelling approach. The project emphasised verification through hand calculations and path-based stress extraction over contour-plot maxima.

CAPSTONE PROJECT

AR-Based CNC Machine Tool Monitoring System

September 2025 – March 2026 · Supervised by Dr Muftooh Siddiqi, Aston University

Designed and developed a fully functional Augmented Reality application for CNC machine tool monitoring and operator training, deployed on the Meta Quest 3 headset. Addresses the absence of immersive, low-cost AR tools for real-time machine data and structured training in manufacturing education.

Five-module interactive training system with OVRInput controller navigation and session persistence via PlayerPrefs

Real-time monitoring panel displaying vibration, temperature, cutting force, and spindle speed at 5 Hz update rate

Fault injection system with colour-coded visual alerts reflecting industrial monitoring conventions

CNC component inspection via menu-driven navigation with sphere indicators (CNCModelHighlighter)

Spatial anchor support for persistent UI panel positioning across sessions

Evaluated using the System Usability Scale (SUS) with fault detection accuracy and response time metrics
